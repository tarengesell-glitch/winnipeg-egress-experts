Common Questions

Everything you wanted to ask before calling.

Why is your price higher than other quotes I'm getting?
Because our quote is the whole project — engineering, permits, inspections, the window, concrete cutting, debris removal, framing, insulation, vapour barrier, drywall, and paint. Cheaper quotes are typically just the cut and the window. Add the rest yourself and the total usually exceeds ours.
I already have a window that was put in without a permit — can you help?
Yes, and it's a common Winnipeg situation. We can assess an existing unpermitted egress window, bring in engineered drawings, and work with the city to get it properly permitted and inspected — or correct the install if it doesn't meet code. It's far cheaper to resolve now than to have it flagged during a home inspection or sale.
How long does the whole project take?
Most single-window projects take 5–7 working days from cut to final paint. We give you a firm schedule before we start.
Do I need a permit for an egress window?
Yes — and we handle it. Cutting a foundation requires engineered drawings and a City of Winnipeg permit. We pull both, attend the inspections, and file the paperwork.
What happens if a contractor skips the permit?
It can become an expensive problem. The city can require unpermitted work to be torn out, re-engineered, and rebuilt. It also shows up in home inspections and can derail a future sale. We don't take that risk — and we don't ask clients to.
Will my basement room count as a legal bedroom?
When sized and installed to code, yes. That's a major reason people call us — a legal bedroom can significantly lift resale value and rental potential.
How much mess does this make?
Cutting concrete is dusty work, but we use dust control, drop sheets, and contained access points. We clean as we go, with a final clean before walk-through. We also haul every bit of concrete and dirt off-site — no piles left behind.
Do you offer a warranty?
Yes — a written workmanship warranty from us, plus the manufacturer's warranty on the window. If anything isn't right, we come back.
